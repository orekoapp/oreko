# Customer Interview Guide: Invoices & Quotes Software

## Interview Overview

**Purpose:** Validate problems, understand workflows, and gather insights from target users to inform product development for invoicing and quoting software.

**Duration:** 45-50 minutes
**Format:** Video call (preferred) or phone
**Recording:** Always request permission

---

## Interview Objectives

1. **Validate Pain Points:** Confirm that invoicing and quoting workflows are genuinely painful for target users
2. **Understand Current Workflows:** Map how users currently create, send, and track invoices and quotes
3. **Identify Severity:** Gauge how severe each problem is (time, money, stress)
4. **Discover Workarounds:** Learn what solutions users have tried and why they failed
5. **Prioritize Features:** Understand must-have vs nice-to-have capabilities
6. **Gauge Willingness to Pay:** Assess price sensitivity and value perception

## Screening Questions

*Use these to qualify interviewees before scheduling the full interview.*

1. "Do you regularly send invoices or quotes to clients?" (Must be YES)
2. "Approximately how many invoices/quotes do you send per month?"
   - Target: 5+ per month
3. "What tools do you currently use for invoicing?"
4. "Are you the primary person responsible for invoicing in your business?"
   - Target: Decision-maker or primary user

**Disqualify if:**
- Sends fewer than 3 invoices per month
- Uses enterprise ERP system (not our target)
- Not involved in invoicing decisions

## Interview Script

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Introduction:**

> "Thank you so much for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm researching how freelancers and small business owners handle their invoicing and quoting processes. I want to be clear upfront - I'm not here to sell you anything. I'm genuinely trying to learn from your experience so we can understand the real challenges people face."

**Permission:**

> "Before we begin, is it okay if I record this conversation? It's just for my notes, and I won't share it with anyone outside our team. You can also ask me to turn it off at any point."

**Format:**

> "This should take about 45 minutes. I'll ask questions about your current workflow and any challenges you face. There are no right or wrong answers - I'm just trying to understand your real experience. Feel free to go off on tangents if something comes to mind."

---

### Warm-Up Questions (5 minutes)

*Goal: Build rapport and understand context.*

1. **"Tell me a bit about your business. What do you do?"**
   - Listen for: Business type, team size, client types

2. **"How long have you been running your business?"**
   - Listen for: Experience level, growth trajectory

3. **"Who are your typical clients?"**
   - Listen for: B2B vs B2C, project size, payment expectations

4. **"Roughly how many invoices or quotes do you send in a typical month?"**
   - Listen for: Volume, frequency, seasonality

---

### Current Workflow Questions (10 minutes)

*Goal: Map their existing process in detail.*

5. **"Walk me through what happens from the moment you agree on work with a client to when you get paid."**
   - Follow-up: "What happens next?"
   - Follow-up: "Who else is involved in that step?"
   - Follow-up: "Can you show me if possible?" (screen share)

6. **"What tools or systems do you use for quotes and invoices?"**
   - Follow-up: "How did you end up using that tool?"
   - Follow-up: "What else did you try before?"

7. **"How do you handle situations where payment terms are complex - like deposits, milestones, or retainers?"**
   - Listen for: Manual workarounds, pain points

8. **"When a quote needs client approval, what does that process look like?"**
   - Follow-up: "How do you know when they've actually agreed?"
   - Follow-up: "Have you ever had disputes about what was agreed?"

9. **"How do you track which invoices are paid, pending, or overdue?"**
   - Follow-up: "How often do you check this?"
   - Follow-up: "What happens when something falls through the cracks?"

---

### Pain Point Exploration (15 minutes)

*Goal: Dig deep into problems and their impact.*

10. **"What's the most frustrating part of your invoicing process?"**
    - Follow-up: "Why is that frustrating?"
    - Follow-up: "Can you give me a specific example?"
    - Follow-up: "How often does this happen?"

11. **"Tell me about a time when something went wrong with an invoice or quote."**
    - Follow-up: "What happened as a result?"
    - Follow-up: "How much time/money did that cost you?"
    - Follow-up: "How did you resolve it?"

12. **"What tasks related to invoicing do you find yourself doing repeatedly that feel like a waste of time?"**
    - Listen for: Manual data entry, copy-paste, recalculating

13. **"How do you handle taxes on your invoices?"**
    - Follow-up: "Have you ever made mistakes with tax calculations?"
    - Follow-up: "How confident are you that your tax handling is correct?"

14. **"What happens when a client wants to negotiate or change a quote?"**
    - Follow-up: "How many back-and-forth exchanges does that typically take?"
    - Follow-up: "Do you keep track of different versions?"

15. **"Have you ever lost money because of issues with your invoicing or quoting process?"**
    - Follow-up: "Tell me more about that."
    - Follow-up: "How much would you estimate?"

16. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how you handle quotes and invoices, what would it be?"**
    - Follow-up: "Why that specifically?"
    - Follow-up: "What would change for you if that was fixed?"

---

### Impact Assessment (5 minutes)

*Goal: Quantify the cost of current problems.*

17. **"How much time do you spend each week on invoicing and quoting tasks?"**
    - Follow-up: "What else could you be doing with that time?"

18. **"Have you ever delayed sending an invoice because the process was too cumbersome?"**
    - Follow-up: "How often does that happen?"
    - Follow-up: "What's the impact on your cash flow?"

19. **"What does chasing late payments look like for you?"**
    - Follow-up: "How often do you have to do this?"
    - Follow-up: "How does that feel?"

20. **"On a scale of 1-10, how satisfied are you with your current invoicing setup?"**
    - Follow-up: "What would need to change to make it a 10?"

---

### Solution Validation (10 minutes)

*Goal: Test specific feature hypotheses without pitching.*

21. **"If a tool could automatically remind clients about upcoming or overdue payments, how valuable would that be to you?"**
    - Follow-up: "How would that change things for you?"

22. **"How do you currently handle situations where clients need to pay in installments?"**
    - Follow-up: "What's challenging about that?"
    - Follow-up: "How would automatic payment scheduling help?"

23. **"What would it mean to you if quotes could automatically convert to invoices once the client approves?"**
    - Follow-up: "What problems would that solve?"

24. **"How important is it for you to accept multiple payment methods?"**
    - Follow-up: "What methods do your clients prefer?"
    - Follow-up: "Have you lost business because of payment method limitations?"

25. **"What information do you typically need to include in your quotes or invoices beyond basic line items?"**
    - Listen for: Contracts, terms, attachments, schedules

**Pricing Exploration (tread carefully):**

26. **"What do you currently pay for invoicing tools?"**
    - Follow-up: "Do you feel you get good value for that?"

27. **"If a tool solved the problems we've discussed, what would that be worth to you?"**
    - Don't push for specific numbers if they're hesitant
    - Listen for: Relative value, comparison to current spend

---

### Wrap-Up (5 minutes)

28. **"What questions did I not ask that I should have?"**
    - Often reveals unexpected insights

29. **"Is there anything else about your invoicing process that you'd like to share?"**

30. **"Who else do you know that struggles with similar challenges? Could you introduce me?"**
    - Request specific introductions, not just "anyone"

31. **"Would you be open to me following up in a few weeks if I have more questions?"**

**Closing:**

> "Thank you so much for your time today. This has been incredibly helpful. I'll send you a brief summary of what I learned, and please don't hesitate to reach out if you think of anything else."

## Interview Tips for the Interviewer

### Do's

1. **Listen more than you talk** - Aim for 80% listening, 20% talking
2. **Ask "why" and "tell me more"** - Dig deeper on interesting threads
3. **Ask about past behavior** - "Tell me about the last time..." not "Would you..."
4. **Embrace silence** - Pause after they finish; they often continue with gold
5. **Take verbatim notes** - Exact quotes are more valuable than paraphrasing
6. **Follow the interviewee's energy** - If they're passionate about something, explore it
7. **Validate emotions** - "That sounds frustrating" builds rapport
8. **Be genuinely curious** - Your interest should be authentic

### Don'ts

1. **Don't pitch your idea** - You're learning, not selling
2. **Don't ask leading questions** - "Wouldn't it be great if..." is leading
3. **Don't ask hypotheticals** - "Would you use..." gets unreliable answers
4. **Don't defend or explain** - Even if they misunderstand something
5. **Don't fill silences too quickly** - Let them think
6. **Don't argue or correct** - Their perception is their reality
7. **Don't take "yes" at face value** - Dig for specifics
8. **Don't ask about price first** - Establish value before discussing cost

### Red Flags (Unreliable Signals)

- "That's a great idea!" - Politeness, not validation
- "I would definitely use that" - Future hypothetical, not commitment
- "Everyone I know would want this" - Proxy opinions are unreliable
- Compliments about you or your questions - They're being nice
- "Just add [feature] and it's perfect" - Feature requests without pain context

### Green Flags (Strong Validation Signals)

- Specific stories with emotional detail
- They've already tried to solve this problem
- They can quantify the cost (time or money)
- They ask when they can buy/use it
- They offer to introduce you to others with the same problem
- They describe workarounds they've built

## Question Bank by Persona

### For Creative Claire (Freelancer/Solo)

- "How do you balance client work with administrative tasks like invoicing?"
- "What happens when you're in the middle of a creative project and need to send an invoice?"
- "How do you handle clients who want to see work before paying?"
- "Do you ever forget to send invoices because you're focused on the creative work?"

### For Contractor Chris (Small Team/Project-Based)

- "How do you handle change orders mid-project?"
- "What's your process for tracking project costs against the original quote?"
- "How do you coordinate invoicing with your team members?"
- "What happens when material costs change after you've sent a quote?"
- "How do you handle deposit and progress billing?"
